Abstract

Relighting involves altering how a scene or object appears under new lighting conditions—a crucial capability for computer vision, graphics, and immersive applications. Despite its potential, high-quality relighting from minimal input remains a significant challenge due to the complexity of lighting interactions and scene geometry. This work introduces two efficient pipelines: SV-GaSRelight and Dance3DRelight, both designed for single-image-based relighting. SV-GaSRelight leverages 3D Gaussian splatting with learned geometric and reflectance priors to generate photorealistic results from a single RGB image, rivaling multi-view methods. Dance3DRelight extends this to full-scene reconstruction and dynamic relighting, combining mesh recovery, intrinsic decomposition, and shader-based rendering—all within a browser-based system. User studies confirm the perceptual realism and robustness of both pipelines across varying lighting conditions. A new dataset and interactive evaluation tool were also developed to support future research and real-time comparison.
